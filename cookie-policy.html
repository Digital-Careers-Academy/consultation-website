What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. These files contain information that helps a website remember user preferences, track usage patterns, and deliver a more seamless and personalized experience. Cookies are widely used across websites to improve efficiency and enhance usability. They can be session-based (deleted after you close your browser) or persistent (stored for a set period to remember your preferences).

Types of Cookies We Use
  • Necessary Cookies

    These cookies are essential for the website to function properly. They enable basic functionalities such as security, network management, and accessibility. You cannot opt out of these cookies as they are necessary for the website to function. Examples include session cookies that keep you logged in and security cookies that protect against fraudulent activities.

  • Performance Cookies

    Performance cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often and if users encounter any errors. The data collected is anonymous and helps us improve the websiteâ€™s performance and user experience. These cookies allow us to refine our services and ensure that our website runs efficiently.

  • Functional Cookies

    Functional cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization. These cookies remember user preferences, such as language settings, font sizes, and other customizable features. By allowing these cookies, users can experience a more tailored website interaction. Disabling these cookies may result in a less customized browsing experience.

  • Analytical & Advertising Cookies

    We use analytical cookies to understand visitor interactions with our website, enabling us to make improvements based on user behavior. These cookies help us gather insights into website traffic, trends, and engagement. Advertising cookies, on the other hand, help us deliver targeted advertisements based on browsing habits. These cookies may track users across multiple websites to deliver relevant marketing content.

  • Third-Party Cookies

    Some cookies on our website may be placed by third-party services we use, such as Google Analytics, social media platforms, and advertising networks. These third parties may collect data about your online activities across different websites, allowing us to provide more personalized services. While we do not control third-party cookies, we ensure that we work only with trusted providers who adhere to data protection regulations. You can manage your third-party cookie preferences through your browser settings.

How to Manage and Delete Cookies

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time by adjusting your browser settings. Most web browsers allow you to block, restrict, or delete cookies. However, please note that disabling certain cookies may impact the functionality of our website, limiting your ability to access some features. To learn more about managing cookies, visit the help section of your browser or refer to third-party websites that provide detailed guidance on cookie management.
